They all share the same passion for chasing the largest winter swells and riding the biggest and heaviest waves in the world. Big wave surfing is nearly a sport within itself. It is addictive and creates adrenaline junkies. Let's put things in perspective. In a 15-second swell period scenario, a 50-foot wave (15 meters) carries about 1.7 MW of power across each meter of a wavefront. As a result, surfing huge waves can be a death sentence for those without the knowledge, physical and mental training, and experience in extreme spots and adverse weather conditions.
